In creating dynamic and interactive web applications, PHP (Hypertext Preprocessor),
is very widely used; it can also be incorporated in HTML.

PHP has a wide range of applications not just website development but also the development of applications, and customer relationship management.
Being one of the first server-side languages, PHP made it simple to add new features to websites without depending on stored information in external files.
Many popular and successful commercial websites also use this language, including Wikipedia, Vimeo, Etsy, Mailchimp, Yahoo, Wordpress, Flickr, and Magento.

Furthermore, it's interesting to note that, according to W3Techs, 77.6% of all websites use PHP for the server-side of their activities.
At this point, it should be clear that developing a website is vital to any genuine attempt at promoting the business.
Without it, building a name for oneself on a global scale would be an incredibly challenging undertaking.
